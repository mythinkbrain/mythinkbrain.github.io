13. keripik apel keripik nangka keripik salak merupakan oleh-oleh khas dari


Keripik apel, keripik nangka dan keripik salak merupakan oleh - oleh khas dari kota Malang, provinsi Jawa Timur.
PEMBAHASAN :

Kota Malang memiliki hasil produksi keripik buah yang cukup melimpah karena banyak dukungan dari UKM yang bergerak di bidang usaha pengolahan keripik dari berbagai macam jenis bahan baku dan salah satunya yang besar adalah produksi kripik buah nangka, apel dan salak.

Struktur alam tropis Indonesia sangat mendukung bagi perkembangan dan pertumbuhan buah. Di tempat yang memiliki curah hujan baik khususnya daerah tropis seperti Indonesia sangat mendukung tanaman untuk bisa berbuah bagus sepanjang tahun, hal ini bisa menjadi acuan positif bagi rumah produksi yang bergerak di bidang keripik buah seperti di kota Malang.

Semenjak diperkenalkannya sistem pengeringan buah di daerah Malang dengan menggunakan alat Vacuum Frying, antusias dari masyarakat untuk mengembangkannya melalui jalur produksi pemanfaatan hasil alam cukup tinggi minatnya, terutama jenis hasil bumi buah salak, nangka dan apel yang cukup melimpah pada masa panen raya tiba bisa tersalurkan dengan baik melalui inovasi baru pengolahan makanan.

18. report text about potato


Potato

The potato is a starchy , tuberous crop from the perennial Solanum tuberosum of the Solanaceae
family (also known as the nightshades). The word may refer to the plant itself as well as the
edible tuber. In the region of the Andes , there are some other closely related cultivated potato
species.
Potatoes were introduced outside the Andes region four centuries ago, and have become
an integral part of much of the world's cuisine. It is the world's fourth-largest food crop,
following rice , wheat and maize . Long-term storage of potatoes requires specialised care in
cold warehouses.
Wild potato species occur throughout the Americas, from the United States to southern Chile.
The potato was originally believed to have been domesticated independently in multiple
locations, but later genetic testing of the wide variety of cultivars and wild species proved a
single origin for potatoes in the area of present-day southern Peru and extreme northwestern
Bolivia (from a species in the Solanum brevicaule complex), where they were domesticated 7,000–
10,000 years ago.
Following centuries of selective breeding , there are now over a
thousand different types of potatoes. Of these subspecies, a variety that at one point grew in
the Chiloé Archipelago (the potato's south-central Chilean sub-center of origin) left its
germplasm on over 99% of the cultivated potatoes worldwide.
The annual diet of an average global citizen in the first decade of the 21st century included
about 33 kg (73 lb) of potato. However, the local importance of potato is extremely variable
and rapidly changing. It remains an essential crop in Europe (especially eastern and central
Europe), where per capita production is still the highest in the world, but the most rapid expansion
over the past few decades has occurred in southern and eastern Asia. China is now the world's
largest potato-producing country, and nearly a third of the world's potatoes are harvested in
China and India.
