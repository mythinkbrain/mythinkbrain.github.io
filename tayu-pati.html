Rani: Hey, what are you guys doing? (as he rushes toward Roni and Udin)
Susi: (follow Rani)
Udin: Eh ..., Rani, Susi.Kalian want to join us to eat here? Good loh atmosphere, eating on the edge of a small river is exciting.
Roni: Yeah ya, our food so it feels more delicious if eaten with a different atmosphere. Do you want to join?
Rani: That's not what I'm trying to ask. Why are you casually throwing your leftovers out of the river? Are you aware of what you are doing?
Susie: Yeah, do you realize that? do you do this can damage the environment and can cause a big disaster?
Roni: Oh ... just now? Hmm yes we throw out our rice paper trays and snack packs in this river. So what? Is there something wrong?
Susi: Yes of course wrong, river is not a place to throw garbage. Is not there a garbage dump? Why do not you throw it in there?
Udin: Hmmm how is Sus? We know that throwing away the samapah here is forbidden. But it's not that commonly done by many people huh? So we're not completely wrong right?
Susi: Yes of course your actions are wrong. Currently the river is very clean and clear, but eventually if dirt with continuous waste will become dirty.
Roni: Hey, come on ... we just throw out a little trash from the rest of our food. In addition we are also not the only ones who throw garbage in the river. Do not blame us so much!
Udin: That's right, you see the trash bin! Far from where we eat. It would be impractical if we had to walk away just to get rid of the trash.
Rani: Din, Ron, still your deeds can not be justified. Although you are not the only one who dumps the garbage in the river. At least starting from yourself, throw the garbage in place. Do not be in the river!
Susie: That's right, even if the trash is far away. Bring your old food waste. Look at us, we still keep our leftover trash in our hands. We are patient to not throw this garbage carelessly. We will throw it in the bin.
Roni and Udin: (scratching the silent thought while listening to Susi and Rani talking)
Rani: Do you know the consequences of your actions?
Roni and Udin: (shook)
Susi: Your actions can lead to major natural disasters that are floods and the thing is certainly you have damaged the environment by doing so.
Roni: That's right Sus? That far?
Susie: yes obviously, if the garbage you dumped has clogged the stream so that the flow will be disturbed, of course, the water will overflow and overwhelm us. Moreover this time the rainy season right? Very potential for flooding. Are you aware?
Rani: That's right. Imagine if there are only 10 people like you who dump garbage in the river assuming one person throw away the garbage 1kg / hari. So there are 10 kg of rubbish dumped in the river every day. Can you imagine that?
Roni: forgive us sus, ran. We will not throw garbage in the river again. From now on we will throw it in the trash that has been provided. Is not it Din?
Udin: Yeah, trust me. We will change our bad habits.
Susie: Okay, I believe. From now on do not throw garbage carelessly again huh!
Rani: Let's keep our environment healthy and do not litter!

28. Tembung kepati-pati tegese​


Tegese tembung kepati-pati yoiku kahanan wong sing wis ora ana pengarepan utawa ana ing cedheke kepaten. Biasane tembung iki digawe kanggo ukara pepindhan.

Pembahasan

Soal diatas merupakan salah satu materi dalam bahasa Jawa. Di mana bahasa Jawa merupakan salah satu bahasa daerah yang ada di Indonesia. Tepatnya di daerah Jawa Timur, Jawa Tengah dan Jogjakarta. Adapun materi yang dimaksud adalah "tembung padha tegese". Kata kepati-pati memiliki arti berupa keadaan seseorang yang sudah putus asa atau berada di ambang akhir sebuah kematian. Biasanya kata kepati-pati digunakan dalam kalimat pengandaian. Karena di dalam kata tersebut terdapat makna yang tersembunyi.
